Infrastructure Layer

The Infrastructure class represents the facility-level support systems that enable the operation, stability, and sustainability of HPC environments. 
Unlike compute or logical resources, this layer focuses on the physical services required to maintain large-scale supercomputing infrastructures efficiently and reliably.

Main subclasses of Infrastructure
CoolingSystem
Represents the systems responsible for maintaining optimal temperature conditions across HPC components and datacenter environments.
This includes cooling technologies that protect hardware performance, reliability, and energy efficiency.
EnergyManagement
Describes the infrastructure and monitoring mechanisms used to control, optimize, and analyze power consumption within the HPC ecosystem.
This layer supports energy-aware operation and sustainability strategies for large-scale computing facilities.
PowerDistributionUnit (PDU)
Models the hardware units responsible for distributing electrical power to racks, nodes, and other infrastructure components.
PDUs also provide metering information that can be used for operational monitoring and metrics such as Power Usage Effectiveness (PUE).
Role within the Ontology

The Infrastructure layer complements both the physical HPC resources and the logical management layers by representing the environmental and operational backbone of the datacenter. Through this abstraction, the ontology is able to capture not only computational capabilities, but also the energy, cooling, and power-management aspects that are essential in modern HPC systems.
